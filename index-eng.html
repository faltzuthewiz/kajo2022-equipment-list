Begin here - general instructions for packing

Please mark down your equipment with your name, your scout group's abbreviation and your camp unit's number, such as Scotty Scouter SGA (Scout Group Abbreviation) 987. Waterproof packing is recommended, such as dry bags and plastic bags.

On the day of arrival, there are hundreds of buses on the campsite. The arriving campers and their equipment are unpacked in a minute detailed timetable. Therefore, pack your equipment firmly and avoid any dangling bundles on the bag. Take only the necessary items on the bus in order to step out quickly and to move the bags out of the bus.

First-timer's top 10 for a large-scale camp
  1. It would be wise to to follow the equipment list since it is written down by large-scale camp veterans.
  2. Bring suitable shoes, because there will be lots of walking on the campsite! The campsite is big and the distances may be long.
  3. In order to keep track of your items, you should mark down all of them with your name.
  4. Eat every time there is food on the table, even when it is not your favourite. You cannot go for long with an empty stomach on a big camp.
  5. Use your chance to swim, and wash up regularly in the showers.
  6. Prepare to queue.
  7. Remember to get enough rest in order to have the energy to enjoy the program all the way to the end of the camp.
  8. Keep an open mind and stay adaptive! Things do not always to go according to plans, and it is a good idea to have a plan B in case there is overcrowding in the program valleys.
  9. Keep your eyes and ears open. There are all kinds of happenings around the campsite.
  10. Enjoy Kajo! Large-scale camps do not happen every year, so remember to have fun and enjoy the ride!

Personal medications
